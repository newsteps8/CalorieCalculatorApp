What is your daily calorie needs?

It consists of three main components: "basal metabolism", "exercise" and "energy in diet". The biggest determinant among them is basal metabolic rate. BMH shows the amount of energy consumed while resting or sleeping, that is, when no activity is shown, and constitutes 60 to 80 percent of the daily calories consumed. Physical activity is also one of the important factors in energy consumption, that is, weight loss or gain. In the event that the physical activity is increased, the rate of metabolism, and therefore the daily calorie requirement, is increased.

How many calories should I take per day?

The amount to be taken daily varies from person to person. The calculator uses the Harris-Benedict principle to detect calories expended in 1 day. The most important factors in this principle are the amount of nutrients taken in daily activities, in addition to the basal metabolic rate calculated based on the person's weight, height and age.

The formula used to find the basal metabolic rate is given below.

In women: 655 + 9.6 X (weight in kg) + 1.8 X (height in cm) - 4.7 X (age)

In men: 66 + 13.7 X (weight in kg) + 5 X (height in cm) - 6.8 X (age)
